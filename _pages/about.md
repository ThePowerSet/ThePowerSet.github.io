I am a **Financial Risk and Data Analysis** graduate student at **Sapienza University of Rome**, with a background in **Economics and Finance** and a strong interest in quantitative finance, financial markets, and machine learning.

---

#### Education

My academic journey began with a **bachelor’s degree in Economics and Finance**, where I developed a strong foundation in econometrics, advanced statistics, monetary policy, financial intermediaries, and European institutions, including the role and functioning of the **European Central Bank**.

I later strengthened this background through my graduate studies, with a deeper focus on quantitative financial modeling, probability theory, stochastic processes, risk measurement, forecasting, derivatives, and credit markets.

#### Research Interests

This path has led me to develop a strong interest in the **mathematical and probabilistic foundations of pricing models**, particularly for derivatives such as options and credit derivatives such as **Credit Default Swaps**.

I am also very interested in **artificial intelligence**, both as a field of study and as a tool to enhance research, coding, and quantitative analysis when used with a consistent workflow, critical judgment, and a solid theoretical foundation.

#### Technical Skills

I have applied my knowledge across different areas of quantitative finance, from term-structure modeling and volatility forecasting to option pricing, hedging, and non-parametric statistical learning.

I primarily work in **Python**, using libraries such as NumPy, Pandas, TensorFlow, scikit-learn, and GPflow. I am also proficient in **R**, have a basic knowledge of **C**, and have experience working in high-performance computing environments.

#### Beyond Finance

Outside of finance and programming, I am interested in free-diving, Muay Thai, chess, hiking, and nature.
