I am a Financial Risk and Data Analysis graduate student at Sapienza University of Rome, with a background in Economics and Finance and a strong interest in quantitative finance, financial markets, and machine learning.

My academic journey began with a bachelor in Economics and Finance, where I developed a strong foundation in econometrics, advanced statistics, monetary policy, financial intermediaries, and European institutions, including the role and functioning of the European Central Bank. I have then strengthened this background through my graduate studies, with a deeper focus on quantitative financial modeling, probability theory, stochastic processes, risk measurement, forecasting, derivatives, and credit markets.

This path has led me to develop a strong interest in the mathematical and probabilistic foundations of pricing models, particularly for derivatives such as options and credit products such as CDS. I am also very interested in artificial intelligence, both as a field of study and as a powerful tool to enhance research, coding, and quantitative analysis when used through a consistent workflow, critical judgment, and a solid theoretical foundation.

I have applied my knowledge across different areas of quantitative finance, from term-structure modeling and volatility forecasting to option pricing, hedging, and non-parametric statistical learning. My technical work is primarily carried out in Python, using libraries such as NumPy, Pandas, TensorFlow, scikit-learn, and GPflow. I am also proficient in R, have a basic knowledge of C, and have experience working in high-performance computing environments.

Beyond finance and programming, I am passionate about free-diving, Muay Thai, chess, hiking, and nature.
